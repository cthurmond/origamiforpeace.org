Nathanael A. Kennedy

for Santa Cruz City Council 2016

My Ideas For Our City Council

  1. I support our police, firefighters & paramedics! They have difficult jobs protecting the public, which is all of us! They're putting their lives on the line every day for us. They all deserve better pay and most of all the best health care plans absolutely possible. Never forget what happened to former paramedic Bryan Stow!

  2. I will fight for the rights of our citizens of the City of Santa Cruz! Everyone from our street artists & vendors, skateboarders, our homeless population, and any others currently being targeted by unfair laws... and I will fight for the rights of our city workers & peace officers. The public in general often feels their voice isn't being heard at our council meetings... I want to help change that!

  3. We must address homelessness in Santa Cruz! We have the River Street and Coral Street shelters but we need more. We must create new shelters and campgrounds that people can legally stay at. As a requirement for utilizing some of these new services, we will provide needed help finding a job as well as finding a real home. Refusal of help finding work and long-term housing will limit the amount of time they are allowed to stay at transitional housing. Becoming homeless is something that can happen overnight, to anyone, even the very wealthy. We could be hit with a big earthquake, tsunami, or any other natural disaster. Your house could burn down or be foreclosed after a job loss. Even if you are a multi-millionaire something drastic can still happen, such as being sued into bankruptcy, etc. Many of us are just “a paycheck away.”

  4. Dogs on Pacific Ave. Yes, it's finally been “legalized” after 37 years. But there are still some overbearing restrictions, most of all the “No Dogs After Dark” ordinance which we need to repeal! We must have plastic bag dispensers attached to all of our trash cans! And if you don't pick up after your dog does it's business then you should get a ticket. There is no poop fairy.

  1. Skateboarding is Not a Crime... unless you are downtown on Pacific Avenue! We should change this law so people on skateboards or skate devices can legally ride while still being held accountable to traffic laws... you should be able to ride a skateboard with all the same restrictions as a bicycle. This law hardly stops anybody! Let's encourage skateboarders to actually obey our traffic laws...

    At least give a good reason for that ticket!

  1. Sidewalk Chalk is Not a Crime. Right now if you use sidewalk chalk on the sidewalk you are subject to a nasty misdemeanor vandalism ticket known as 9.20.010mc. This law is in place for a reason... but it's misguided. First we need to decriminalize chalk, so it's merely an infraction like a speeding ticket. One of the best ways to handle this is to have specific areas, specific “squares” on the sidewalk clearly marked with signs explaining these new laws. Before designating legally-approved chalk-art areas, we should consult with adjoining businesses and get approval first. Another possibility is to require a chalking license or permit, issued by our local police department for something like $10, allowing the use of “temporary” chalking areas, that can be designated anywhere in the city.

  2. The Recreational Vehicle (RV) Ban. Recently passed, there is now a ban on parking an RV overnight anywhere in the City of Santa Cruz. Even for homeowners & residents whom own an RV, it must be moved at least once every 24 hours, unless you're lucky enough to have plenty of private property parking for your vehicle(s). This is more than simply just a “War on the Well-off Poor”, it is also a “War on the Wealthy” as well, those that own RV's that can sometimes be worth a 6-figure amount, that can often be worth a lot more than some houses. RV's should legally be considered the same as any other vehicle (of equal size) regarding parking rights. This is driving away a lot of (often very wealthy) tourists & even residents, many of whom are a backbone of our local economy... and what about when someone rather famous is performing in town? Are we going to tell Tom Petty he's got to leave? We must repeal this ridiculous RV ban!

  3. We must protect our First Amendment constitutional rights, and also our rights for the use of public space (think outside the blue boxes), especially for our local street artists & vendors! Use of these “new” boxes currently designated on our sidewalk should NOT be mandatory, and anywhere someone could legally setup before we had these boxes should still be legal now, with the boxes being “guaranteed” legal locations. The current time limit of hour needs to be increased. It can be a real inconvenience to have to continually move a table/setup so frequently. We need a new time limit of no less than three hours, and we should have paid permits available (maybe even taxes) for allowing the sale of any items other than the “artwork” currently permitted. We need to allow the sale of anything (that is not illegal contraband), so long as a permit has been granted.

  4. We must create a new downtown flea market to be open on weekends, similar to the current “Farmer's Market”, allowing vendors to sell anything (that's not illegal!) by creating the needed space with street closures and the use of adjacent parking lots. Sadly, the days are numbered for our regular favorite Santa Cruz Flea Market.

  5. We need more public bathrooms and portable restrooms available 24/7! When nature calls you never want to put her on hold! In addition, for public safety and preventing the spread of disease, we need drop boxes for used sharps and needles in all our public restrooms. While we cannot stop the use of illegal drugs, we can prevent the spread of diseases by allowing proper disposal of bio-hazardous materials.
